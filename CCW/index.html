Training your Mind and Body

The Trinity of Tae Kwon Do consists of the three major aspects of Tae Kwon Do; the Body, the Mind, and the Spirit. In order for an individual to develop into a complete and well-rounded person, he must cultivate each aspect of Tae Kwon Do. If only one or two of the aspects are developed, then a person, no matter how hard he trains, may never become a true Master of Art of Tae Kwon Do.

A huge thanks to Year Up Atlanta allowing us to use the facility to teach others the basics of Tae Kwon do and assisting them in concentration techniques that will aid them in class and work.

Our instruction, mission and vision are all based on the five tenets of TKD: Courtesy, Integrity, Perseverance, Self-Control, and Indomitable Spirit.

Learning at Concentraining

We teach Tae Kwon Do with dignity and respect of all students regardless of age, belt rank, gender, race or culture. Learning is best in a positive,supportive environment. Students progress through the rank system learning that not only are positive efforts rewared, but that added rewards also mean added responsibilites to themselves and others. Our instruction, mission and vision are all based on the five tenets of TKD: Courtesy, Intergrity, Perseverance, Self-Control, and Indomitable Spirit.

Our curriculum is equally balanced between conditioning and fitness, basic tecniques, self-defense, forms, sparring and the mental/non physical attributes of TKD.
